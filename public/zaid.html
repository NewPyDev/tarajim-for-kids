Zaid ibn Thabit

The Scribe - Compiler of the Quran

The Life of Zaid ibn Thabit

Birth and Early Life

Zaid ibn Thabit was born in 610 CE in Medina. He belonged to the Banu Najjar clan of the Khazraj tribe. When the Prophet migrated to Medina, young Zaid was only eleven years old. Despite his youth, he showed exceptional intelligence and quickly learned to read and write, which was rare at that time. The Prophet recognized his talents and took him under his personal care.

Becoming the Prophet's Scribe

Due to his literacy and sharp mind, Zaid became the chief scribe of the Prophet Muhammad. He was responsible for writing down the Quranic revelations as they were revealed. The Prophet also asked him to learn Hebrew and Syriac languages to help with correspondence with Jewish and Christian communities. Zaid mastered these languages in just a few weeks, demonstrating his remarkable intellectual abilities.

Compilation of the Quran

After the Prophet's death, during the caliphate of Abu Bakr, Zaid was chosen to compile the Quran into a single book. This was a monumental task that required collecting verses from various written materials and the memories of the companions. Later, during Uthman's caliphate, Zaid led the committee that produced the standardized version of the Quran that is used today. His meticulous work ensured the preservation of the divine message for all future generations.

Legacy and Passing

Zaid continued to serve the Muslim community as a scholar and judge after the compilation of the Quran. He was known for his deep knowledge of Islamic law and his fair judgments. He passed away in 665 CE in Medina at the age of 55. His greatest legacy is the preservation of the Quran in its complete and authentic form, making him one of the most important figures in Islamic history.
