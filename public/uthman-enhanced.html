Birth and Early Life

Uthman ibn Affan was born in 576 CE in Mecca to the wealthy Umayyad clan of the Quraysh. He was known for his exceptional beauty and was nicknamed "Uthman the Handsome." A successful merchant, he was among the wealthiest men in Mecca. He married Ruqayyah, the Prophet's daughter, making him the Prophet's son-in-law.

Embracing Islam

Uthman was among the earliest converts to Islam, accepting the message through Abu Bakr's invitation. Despite facing severe persecution, he remained steadfast. After his wife Ruqayyah's death, he married her sister Umm Kulthum, earning the title "Dhun-Nurayn" (Possessor of the Two Lights) for marrying two daughters of the Prophet.

Contributions to Islam

As the third Caliph (644-656 CE), Uthman oversaw the compilation of the Quran into a single, authoritative manuscript. He expanded the Muslim empire to include North Africa, Cyprus, and parts of Central Asia. Known for his generosity, he used his personal wealth to support the Muslim community. He established the first Muslim navy and expanded the Prophet's Mosque in Medina. His reign saw the establishment of the first Islamic coinage system.

Legacy

Uthman's greatest legacy is the standardized Quran that Muslims use today. He ensured that all copies of the Quran were consistent and authentic. Despite facing challenges during his later years, his contributions to Islamic administration, expansion, and the preservation of the Quran remain unparalleled. He was martyred in 656 CE while reading the Quran, becoming the first martyr in Islamic history.
