A Legacy of Knowledge and Innovation

For over 1,400 years, Islamic civilization has contributed countless innovations, discoveries, and ideas that have shaped our modern world. From mathematics and medicine to art and architecture, Muslim scholars, scientists, and inventors have left an incredible legacy that continues to benefit humanity today.

Mathematics & Numbers

Islamic mathematicians revolutionized how we understand and use numbers, creating systems we still use today.

Amazing Contributions:

  • Introduced the number zero to Europe
  • Developed algebra (from Arabic "al-jabr")
  • Created the decimal system
  • Advanced geometry and trigonometry
  • Invented algorithms (named after Al-Khwarizmi)

Medical Breakthroughs:

A Continuing Legacy

These contributions show how Islamic civilization has been a bridge between cultures, preserving ancient knowledge while creating new discoveries. Today, we continue to benefit from these innovations in our schools, hospitals, technology, and daily lives. The spirit of learning, discovery, and sharing knowledge remains an important part of Islamic values.
