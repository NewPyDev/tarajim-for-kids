Bilal ibn Rabah (رضي الله عنه)

Birth and Early Life

Bilal ibn Rabah was born in Mecca around 580 CE. He was born into slavery and served as a slave to Umayyah ibn Khalaf, who was known for his strong opposition to Islam.

Acceptance of Islam

Bilal was among the earliest converts to Islam. Despite facing severe persecution and torture from his master, he remained steadfast in his faith, repeatedly declaring "Ahad! Ahad!" (One! One!) referring to the oneness of Allah.

Major Contributions to Islam

After being freed by Abu Bakr As-Siddiq, Bilal became one of the closest companions of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. He was chosen as the first muezzin (caller to prayer) in Islam, known for his beautiful and powerful voice. He called the adhan from the top of the Kaaba when Mecca was conquered.

Legacy and Death

Bilal passed away in Damascus around 640 CE. His legacy as the first muezzin and his unwavering faith in the face of persecution continue to inspire Muslims worldwide. His story represents the Islamic principle of equality and the rejection of racial discrimination.
