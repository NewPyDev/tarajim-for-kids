Uthman ibn Affan (رضي الله عنه)

Birth and Early Life

Uthman ibn Affan (رضي الله عنه) was born in 576 CE in Mecca into the wealthy and noble Umayyad clan of the Quraysh tribe. He was known for his modesty, generosity, and honesty. Even before Islam, Uthman was a successful merchant who used his wealth to help others.

How He Entered Islam

Uthman was among the early converts to Islam, accepting the faith after Abu Bakr introduced him to the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. He married Ruqayya, the Prophet's daughter, and later, after her death, married Umm Kulthum, another daughter of the Prophet, earning him the title "Dhun-Nurayn" (Possessor of Two Lights).

Major Contributions to Islam

As the third Caliph, Uthman's greatest contribution was the standardization of the Quran's text. He ordered the compilation of an official version and sent copies to different parts of the growing Islamic empire. He also expanded the Prophet's Mosque in Medina and improved the empire's naval forces.

Legacy and Death

Uthman was known for his generosity and modest lifestyle despite his wealth. He was martyred in 656 CE while reading the Quran. His legacy includes his crucial role in preserving the Quran and his exemplary character as a leader who lived simply despite having access to wealth.
