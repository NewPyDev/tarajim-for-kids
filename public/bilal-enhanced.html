Bilal ibn Rabah

The First Muezzin

Bilal ibn Rabah was born into slavery in Mecca, but his story became one of the most inspiring examples of faith triumphing over oppression. When he accepted Islam, his master Umayyah ibn Khalaf subjected him to severe torture, placing heavy rocks on his chest under the scorching desert sun and demanding he renounce his faith. But Bilal would only repeat 'Ahad, Ahad' (One, One), affirming his belief in the oneness of Allah. Abu Bakr eventually bought and freed him, and Bilal became one of the Prophet's closest companions.
