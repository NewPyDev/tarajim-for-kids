Bilal ibn Rabah

The First Muezzin of Islam

The Voice of Faith

Early Life in Slavery

Bilal was born in Abyssinia (modern-day Ethiopia) and was brought to Makkah as a slave. His master, Umayyah ibn Khalaf, was a cruel man who tortured Bilal when he learned that Bilal had accepted Islam. Bilal was made to lie on the hot desert sand with heavy stones on his chest, but he never gave up his faith. He kept saying "Ahad, Ahad" (One, One), meaning Allah is One.

Freedom and Islam

When Abu Bakr As-Siddiq learned about Bilal's torture, he bought him and set him free. Bilal became one of the earliest Muslims and a close companion of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). He was known for his beautiful, deep voice and his strong faith. When the Muslims migrated to Madinah, Bilal went with them and continued to serve Islam with dedication.

The First Muezzin

When the Prophet needed someone to call people to prayer, he chose Bilal because of his beautiful voice and strong faith. Bilal became the first muezzin (caller to prayer) in Islam. His voice was so melodious that people would come from far away just to hear the adhan (call to prayer). He called the adhan for every prayer during the Prophet's lifetime and was known for his sincerity and devotion.

Later Life and Legacy

After the Prophet's death, Bilal was so sad that he could no longer stay in Madinah. He moved to Syria and continued to serve Islam there. When the Muslims conquered Jerusalem, Bilal climbed to the top of the mosque and gave the adhan there. He died in Damascus around 20 AH (640 CE) at the age of 60. His grave is in Damascus, and Muslims remember him as a symbol of patience, faith, and the beautiful voice that called people to worship Allah.
