Sa'ad ibn Abi Waqqas

The Archer - Conqueror of Persia

The Life of Sa'ad ibn Abi Waqqas

Birth and Early Life

Sa'ad ibn Abi Waqqas was born in 595 CE in Mecca. He belonged to the Banu Zuhrah clan of the Quraysh tribe, making him a maternal uncle of the Prophet Muhammad. Known for his exceptional archery skills from a young age, he was one of the first people to accept Islam when he was only seventeen years old. His early conversion made him one of the youngest companions of the Prophet.

Embracing Islam

Sa'ad was among the first ten people to accept Islam, converted by Abu Bakr. His mother was initially opposed to his conversion and threatened to starve herself unless he renounced Islam. However, Sa'ad remained steadfast in his faith, and eventually, his mother accepted his decision. The Prophet praised Sa'ad's dedication and said about him: "This is my maternal uncle; let every man show me his maternal uncle like this one."

Military Achievements

Sa'ad was renowned as the best archer among the companions. He participated in all major battles alongside the Prophet, including Uhud, where the Prophet gave him his own arrows and said, "Shoot, Sa'ad! May my father and mother be sacrificed for you!" As a military commander, he led the conquest of Iraq and defeated the Sassanian Empire at the Battle of Qadisiyyah in 636 CE. He was also one of the ten companions promised Paradise by the Prophet.

Later Life and Passing

After his military successes, Sa'ad served as the governor of Kufa in Iraq. He was known for his just governance and piety. In his later years, he withdrew from political affairs and focused on worship and teaching. Sa'ad passed away in 674 CE in Medina at the age of 79, being the last of the ten promised Paradise to die. He was buried in Al-Baqi cemetery in Medina, and his legacy as a brave warrior and faithful companion continues to inspire Muslims.
