Umar ibn Al-Khattab (رضي الله عنه)

Birth and Early Life

Umar ibn Al-Khattab (رضي الله عنه) was born in 584 CE in Mecca. He grew up in a family known for their strength and leadership. Umar was tall, strong, and known for his exceptional speaking skills. Before Islam, Umar worked as a merchant and was respected by the Quraysh for his intelligence and bravery.

How He Entered Islam

Initially, Umar was a fierce opponent of Islam and even planned to kill the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. However, one day, he overheard his sister reciting verses from the Quran, and his heart softened. He went straight to the Prophet and accepted Islam. His conversion was a turning point for Muslims, as they gained the courage to practice their religion openly.

Major Contributions to Islam

Umar was instrumental in strengthening the Muslim community. He participated in major battles, including Badr, Uhud, and the Battle of the Trench. As the second Caliph, Umar expanded the Islamic empire to Persia, Egypt, and Syria. He established a welfare system and set up an organized government to administer justice and fairness.

Legacy and Death

Umar was known for his humility and concern for his people. He often roamed the streets at night to check on the welfare of his citizens. Umar was assassinated in 644 CE while leading prayers. His leadership remains a timeless example of justice and piety.
