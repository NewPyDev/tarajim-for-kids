Birth and Early Life

Fatimah bint Muhammad was born in 605 CE as the youngest daughter of Prophet Muhammad and Khadijah. She was known as Az-Zahra (The Radiant One) and held a very special place in her father's heart.

üíñ Her Beautiful Life

Fatimah married Ali ibn Abi Talib and became the mother of Hassan and Hussein, the Prophet's beloved grandsons. Despite facing many hardships including poverty, she remained patient and devoted to her family. She was known for her incredible generosity, often giving away her own food to help the needy even when her family was hungry.

üëë Her Special Status

The Prophet said that Fatimah would be the leader of women in Paradise. She was a perfect example of how to be a devoted daughter, loving wife, and caring mother while maintaining strong faith. Her life showed that women can be leaders and examples for others, balancing family responsibilities with spiritual devotion.

"Allah is pleased when a mother is pleased, and Allah is displeased when a mother is displeased."

Fatimah passed away in 632 CE at the young age of 27, just six months after her father's death. Though her life was short, she left a lasting legacy as a model of patience, generosity, and devotion. She is remembered as the mother of the Prophet's lineage and a perfect example of Islamic womanhood.
